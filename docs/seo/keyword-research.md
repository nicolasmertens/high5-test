## Methodology

Search volume estimates are approximated using publicly available data patterns, competitor analysis (16personalities.com, truity.com, personalitymax.com, discprofile.com), and Google SERP feature observations. Exact volumes fluctuate monthly; directionality matters more than precision. Difficulty ratings (low/medium/high/very high) reflect the competitive landscape for a new domain with zero authority.

**Landing page targets reference our current site structure:**
- `/free-strengths-test` — Strengths landing page
- `/free-disc-test` — DISC landing page
- `/free-enneagram-test` — Enneagram landing page
- `/free-personality-test` — 16 Personalities landing page
- `/blog/*` — Existing blog posts
- `(NEW)` — Requires new content to be created

**Priority definitions:**
- **P1** — Target immediately. High-volume, clear intent match, existing or near-term content opportunity.
- **P2** — Target in next sprint. Moderate volume or needs new content page.
- **P3** — Long-tail opportunity. Lower volume but low difficulty. Build over time.

## SEO Strategy Notes

1. **SERP feature optimization.** Many of our target keywords trigger featured snippets and "People also ask" boxes. Structure content with clear H2/H3 headings, concise paragraph answers (40-60 words) immediately after headings, and FAQ sections with schema markup.

2. **Internal linking.** Every new blog post should cross-link to all four landing pages and at least two other blog posts. The comparison posts are especially powerful for internal linking because they naturally reference multiple frameworks.

3. **URL strategy.** Our `/free-{framework}-test` URL structure is strong for transactional keywords. Blog posts should use descriptive slugs that match the target keyword (e.g., `/blog/disc-vs-enneagram` for "DISC vs Enneagram").

4. **Competitive positioning.** 16personalities.com dominates "16 personalities" and "free personality test" but has thin content on comparisons and cross-framework topics. This is our wedge — we own the multi-framework angle because we offer all four frameworks in one test.

5. **Long-tail accumulation.** Our domain has zero authority. We will not rank for "free personality test" (110K/mo) in month one. The strategy is: publish long-tail content first (P2/P3 keywords with low difficulty), build authority through SERP features and internal links, then gradually compete for P1 keywords.

6. **Schema markup.** Add FAQ schema to all landing pages and blog posts. Add `HowTo` schema for "how to" queries. Add `SoftwareApplication` schema to landing pages.

7. **Every page needs:** unique title tag (under 60 chars), unique meta description (under 155 chars), H1 matching the primary keyword, canonical URL, and Open Graph metadata.
