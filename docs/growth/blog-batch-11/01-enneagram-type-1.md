## Who Is Enneagram Type 1?

Enneagram Type 1 — the Reformer — is principled, purposeful, and driven by an internal standard of rightness. Ones do not just want things to go well; they want things to be done *correctly*. Their sense of integrity is not performative — it comes from a deep conviction that improvement matters and that they are personally responsible for contributing to it.

At their best, Ones are wise, ethical, and inspiring. They hold the line when others bend it and make the people around them better by the example they set. At their most stressed, the same drive becomes rigidity, self-criticism, and frustration at a world that refuses to meet their standard.

## Core Motivation and Fear

**Core desire:** To be good, ethical, and to do the right thing.

**Core fear:** Being corrupt, defective, or morally wrong.

This core fear drives a relentless inner critic — the internal "judge" that evaluates every action against an ideal. The voice can be motivating in healthy doses and punishing when unchecked.

## 5 Strengths of Enneagram Type 1

**1. Uncompromising integrity**
Ones keep their word because they hold themselves to their own standard, not because someone is watching.

**2. Eye for what needs improvement**
Ones see problems before others do — not from pessimism but from a genuine drive to make things better.

**3. Strong moral compass**
When ethical decisions are difficult, Ones have usually already worked through the question. They don't bend under pressure when principles are involved.

**4. Self-discipline**
Ones follow through on commitments even when motivation fades. Their standards are self-generated.

**5. Honest, direct feedback**
When a One gives you feedback, it is real. They are not managing your feelings — they want to help you get it right.

## 5 Weaknesses and Growth Paths

**1. Perfectionism that blocks completion**
*Growth path:* Practice releasing at 90%. Ask: does this serve its purpose? Ship it.

**2. A punishing inner critic**
*Growth path:* Treat yourself with the patience you would extend to a friend. The critic is a voice, not a verdict.

**3. Difficulty resting**
*Growth path:* Reframe rest as part of quality work. Sustainable output requires recovery.

**4. Judging others by your own standard**
*Growth path:* Most people are doing their best by their own logic. Separate your standards from your assessment of others' worth.

**5. Repressed anger expressed as resentment**
*Growth path:* Anger is legitimate information, not a moral failure. Name it directly before it leaks sideways.

## Wings

**1w2 — The Advocate:** Warmer, more relational. The drive to improve things focuses on helping and advocating for others. More emotionally invested in people and causes.

**1w9 — The Idealist:** Calmer, more philosophical. Pursues ideals with patience rather than urgency. Often teachers, writers, and long-range thinkers.

## Famous Type 1 Examples

- **Mahatma Gandhi** — A principled, disciplined moral movement sustained over decades
- **Michelle Obama** — Known for high standards, meticulous preparation, and using her platform for social good
- **Nelson Mandela** — Maintained his values through 27 years of imprisonment; emerged focused on building rather than vengeance
- **Tina Fey** — A perfectionist writer who applies sharp ethical standards and high editorial craft to comedy

## Growth Direction

Type 1 grows toward the positive qualities of Type 7 — spontaneity, joy, and lightness. A healthy One learns that imperfection is not failure, that the world does not depend on their vigilance alone, and that goodness can coexist with delight.

The invitation: hold your ideals with less grip.
