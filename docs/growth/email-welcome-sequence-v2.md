## Email 3: Use Your Results at Work (Day 5)

**Subject:** How to use your personality results at work
**Preview text:** Your manager would benefit from knowing this about you.

Hey,

Your personality results aren't just interesting — they're practical. Here are three ways to use them at work this week:

**1. Share your DISC style with your manager.**
Frame it as: "I work best when I have [autonomy/context/data/time]. Here's what my DISC profile says about my communication style."

**2. Adjust your communication to your team.**
If your colleague is a high-S (patient, steady), give them time and context. If they're a high-D (direct, results-focused), get to the point.

**3. Identify your energy drains.**
Your personality profile shows what drains you. If your top strength is Strategic but you spend 80% of your time in meetings, you're in the wrong role structure.

## Email 4: The Relationship Angle (Day 8)

**Subject:** What your personality type means for your relationships
**Preview text:** The #1 conflict pattern and how to break it.

Hey,

The most common relationship conflict isn't about money, chores, or time. It's about communication styles.

If you're a Thinking type (16 Personalities) and your partner is a Feeling type, you'll approach every disagreement differently. You want to analyze the problem. They want to feel heard first.

Neither is wrong. But if you don't understand the difference, you'll have the same argument forever.

**One question that prevents most personality-based conflicts:**
"Before we talk about this — do you want me to listen, or do you want me to help solve it?"

If the answer doesn't match your current job, don't panic. Small changes — more autonomy, different project types, adjusted communication patterns — can make a big difference.
