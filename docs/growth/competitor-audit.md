# Competitor Audit: Top 5 Personality Test Sites

Author: Head of Growth Agent, 1Test
Date: 2026-04-10
Status: Complete

---

## Executive Summary

The personality test market is dominated by 16personalities.com (1.52B+ tests) and Truity (50M+ users), both operating a freemium model with gated premium reports. PersonalityMax offers genuinely free results but with a poor UX. Crystal (crystalknows.com) targets B2B/sales teams. DISCprofile.com is a legacy DISC-only vendor with limited digital presence.

1Test's wedge is clear: **multi-framework assessment in one sitting** (DISC + Enneagram + Strengths + 16 Personalities) with generous free results. No competitor offers this. Every major player is single-framework or sells separate tests. This is our differentiation.

## 1. 16personalities.com

**URL:** 16personalities.com
**Owner:** NERIS Analytics Limited (founded 2011)
**Scale:** 1.52B+ total tests taken, 733K+ daily, 194M+ in US alone

### Pricing

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Personality test + basic type description (they admit free results show only ~5% of available insights) |
| Premium Career Suite | $29 one-time | 40+ page career guide, 5 AI career mentors, career values/burnout/job preferences assessments |
| Pro Suite | Not publicly listed (higher) | Guides for all 16 types (personal growth, careers, relationships), 32 tests and tools |
| Team Assessments | Calculator-based (not shown) | Team reports, self-directed workshops, per-seat pricing |
| Professionals | Not listed | Bulk reports for coaches/therapists |

### Funnel

1. Free test (10 min, ~100 questions)
2. Basic type description (e.g., "You are an INTJ-A, The Architect")
3. Brief free sections on strengths, weaknesses, relationships, career
4. Aggressive upsell to Premium ($29): "You've only seen 5% of what we can show you"
5. Results page has persistent CTA for Premium Career Suite
6. Team Assessments as separate B2B funnel

### UX Hooks

- Massive social proof: 1.52B+ tests counter, 91.2% accuracy rating, testimonials
- Shareable type badges (e.g., "INTJ-A" — highly viral on social media)
- Email capture via account creation (required to save results)
- 49 languages supported — global reach
- Type-specific communities and content
- Animated type illustrations (distinctive visual identity)
- NPQE spinoff product (story-based personality exploration)
- Substack newsletters by type (e.g., "Leadership by 16Personalities")

### Weaknesses

- **Single framework only.** No DISC, Enneagram, Strengths, or Big Five — just 16 Personalities
- **Free results are aggressively gated.** Their own copy admits only 5% of insights are free — users feel nickel-andimed
- **No multi-framework comparison.** Can't see how your 16P type maps to DISC or Enneagram
- **No relationship matching for free.** Relationship compatibility is gated behind Premium
- **B2B experience is bolt-on.** Team Assessments feel like an afterthought vs consumer product
- **Test is long.** ~100 questions, 10+ minutes commitment
- **No real-time results.** Must complete entire test before seeing anything

### SEO Position

- Dominates "16 personalities" (branded), "free personality test", "personality test", "INTJ", "ENFP", all 16 type names
- Owns featured snippets for type-related queries
- Massive domain authority from 1.52B+ tests + 49 language versions = enormous backlink profile
- Year-over-year content: type descriptions rank for long-tail career/relationship queries

### Opportunity for 1Test

- **Multi-framework is our wedge.** 16P only offers one lens. 1Test gives DISC + Enneagram + Strengths + 16 Personalities in one test, one results page. This is something 16P literally cannot do without rebuilding their product.
- **More generous free results.** 16P's "you've only seen 5%" is frustrating. 1Test should give meaningful free results across all frameworks, with a paywall for deep-dive reports.
- **Relationship insights from shared results.** 16P gates compatibility behind paid. 1Test can offer free compatibility matching when two people share results — a viral loop.
- **SEO counter-position.** Target "16 Personalities vs DISC", "personality test that includes DISC", "free multi-framework personality test" — queries 16P can't rank for because they only offer one framework.

## 2. Truity (truity.com)

**URL:** truity.com
**Owner:** Truity Psychometrics LLC
**Scale:** 50M+ users, 148K+ Enneagram tests/month, 137K+ TypeFinder tests/month

### Pricing

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free (each test) | $0 | Basic results overview for each test |
| Premium Report | ~$29 per test | Full in-depth report (specific price varies by test) |
| Truity@Work | Per-credit | Team/coach assessments, group reporting, dashboard |
| API | Contact | Personality test API for integration |
| TrueYou App | Free | Mobile app for personality tests |

### Funnel

1. Free test (varies: Enneagram 105 questions, TypeFinder 130 questions, DISC 38 questions, Big Five ~100)
2. Brief free results (type overview + basic description)
3. Upsell to premium report ($29) for full analysis, PDF, career info
4. Account creation (email) to save results
5. Cross-sell: "Take another test!" after each result
6. B2B funnel through Truity@Work landing page

### UX Hooks

- Multiple test types on one platform (Enneagram, TypeFinder/16 Personalities, Big Five, DISC, Career, Love Styles, EQ)
- "As Seen In" logos (Inc., CNBC, Bloomberg, Huffpost, etc.) — strong trust signals
- Medically reviewed badge on tests (Dr. Steven Melendy, PsyD)
- Customer reviews (4.9/5 stars)
- TrueYou mobile app
- Newsletter
- Active blog (personality content marketing)
- Affiliate program for distribution
- API for developers

### Weaknesses

- **Tests are separate, not integrated.** You take Enneagram, then DISC separately. No unified profile connecting results across frameworks. This is their biggest gap.
- **Long tests.** 105 questions for Enneagram, 130 for TypeFinder — significant drop-off risk
- **Free results are thin.** Just an overview, no depth. Premium required for anything useful
- **Uses MBTI trademark explicitly** — "Myers-Briggs Type Indicator", "MBTI®" — which limits their copy and creates legal risk. They have a disclaimer but lean into the association
- **No shareable results.** Free tier doesn't include shareable badges/cards
- **UX feels clinical.** Design is functional but not delightful
- **Cross-sell is aggressive** — after every test, you're pushed to take another. Feels like a test mill

### SEO Position

- Strong for "Enneagram test" (#1 or top 3), "DISC test", "Big Five test", "career personality test", "personality test free"
- Blog ranks for informational queries: "What is DISC", "Enneagram types", "personality types"
- Each test page is optimized for its specific keyword cluster
- 50M+ users = strong domain authority and backlinks
- Affiliate program extends reach to partner sites

### Opportunity for 1Test

- **Unified results page.** Truity makes you take 4 separate tests to get 4 frameworks. 1Test gives all 4 in one sitting with a unified results view. This is our #1 differentiator in copy.
- **Cross-framework insights.** "Your ENFJ type aligns with DISC Influencer — here's what that means together." Truity can't do this.
- **Shorter test experience.** 1Test should aim for <15 minutes total for all 4 frameworks. Truity's Enneagram alone is 105 questions.
- **Relationship reports.** Truity has a separate Love Styles test. 1Test can offer integrated relationship compatibility across all frameworks.
- **Target Truity's blog gaps.** Write content like "DISC vs Enneagram vs 16 Personalities — which should you take?" and "personality test that includes multiple frameworks"

## 3. PersonalityMax (personalitymax.com)

**URL:** personalitymax.com
**Owner:** Btb Web AG (Switzerland)
**Scale:** 5,541,250 tests taken since 2007

### Pricing

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Everything — 20-page report, all assessments, no registration |
| Premium | N/A | No premium tier exists |

**Monetization method unclear.** No ads visible on the test page, no premium, no B2B offering. Possible data collection or affiliate traffic monetization.

### Funnel

1. Choose which assessments to include (Personality Type, Multiple Intelligences, Learning Styles, Big Five, Enneagram, Brain Hemispheres)
2. Complete all selected assessments in one long session (estimated 59 minutes for full battery)
3. Instantly receive 20-page report — no paywall, no email required
4. Option to print or share, but no account creation required

### UX Hooks

- **No registration, no email.** Lowest friction entry of any competitor. True anonymity.
- Comprehensive free results (20 pages, 36 charts/graphs)
- Multiple frameworks in one test (closest to 1Test's model)
- Career matches included
- Print-friendly format
- 5.5M+ tests taken social proof
- "Formerly $39, now free" positioning creates perceived value

### Weaknesses

- **Extremely long test.** 59 minutes estimated for full battery — massive drop-off risk
- **Outdated UI/UX.** Site design looks like 2010. Not mobile-optimized. Clip-art style graphics
- **No account system.** No way to save, revisit, or update results. No retention loop whatsoever
- **No B2B/team features.** Individual-only product
- **No blog or content.** Zero content marketing strategy. Minimal SEO footprint
- **No shareable social results.** No badges, cards, or social sharing options
- **No community or engagement features.** Test -> results -> leave. No reason to return
- **No premium tier.** No revenue model visible — unclear sustainability
- **Questionable formatting.** Long scrolling pages, no progress indicators visible in the test UI
- **Low domain authority.** Minimal backlinks compared to 16P and Truity

### SEO Position

- Ranks for "free personality test" (moderately), "free MBTI test" (they reference MBTI in their copy)
- Very weak content footprint — no blog, no type descriptions, no articles
- Relies primarily on organic search for "free personality test" and word of mouth
- Low domain authority vs leaders

### Opportunity for 1Test

- **Validates the multi-framework model.** PersonalityMax proves demand for comprehensive, multi-framework results in one test. But they do it poorly — 59 min, bad UX, no retention.
- **1Test is PersonalityMax but modern.** Same concept (multiple frameworks in one sitting) but with: shorter test time, modern UI, shareable results, account-based retention, mobile-first design, and a sustainable business model.
- **Steal their "free comprehensive results" positioning.** PersonalityMax's claim is "100% free, 20-page report, no registration." 1Test can match this generosity in free tier while also offering a premium upgrade path (which PersonalityMax lacks).
- **Target "free personality test no registration" and "free comprehensive personality test"** — these are PersonalityMax's keywords, and 1Test can outrank them with better content and higher-quality backlinks.

## 4. Crystal (crystalknows.com)

**URL:** crystalknows.com
**Owner:** Crystal Knows Inc.
**Scale:** 1M+ DISC assessments per year. G2 Leader badges. Enterprise clients (Adobe, Verizon, Amazon, Randstad).

### Pricing

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | DISC assessment, basic personality profile |
| Premium | $49/year ($59/month option) | Full personality profile, PDF download, personalized learning path |
| Business/Teams | Contact sales | Team assessments, CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot), hiring tools, dedicated CSM |
| Personality AI | Contact sales | API for predicting personality from LinkedIn/email data |
| Data Plan | Custom | Personality data enrichment via API |

### Funnel

1. Free DISC assessment (28 questions, ~5 minutes)
2. Basic personality profile with DISC type, trait spectrums, brief description
3. Upsell to Premium ($49/yr) for full profile, PDF, learning path
4. B2B: Free demo request → sales-led funnel for Teams/Sales/Hiring products
5. Personality predictions for others (proprietary — predicts DISC type from public data)

### UX Hooks

- **Personality predictions for others.** Crystal's unique differentiator — it predicts other people's DISC type from LinkedIn profiles, email addresses. Used for sales outreach ("write an email that matches their DISC type")
- CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot, CSV enrichment)
- DISC-focused assessments also available for Enneagram, 16 Personalities, Big Five
- Shareable profile pages with communication tips
- G2 badges prominently displayed (Leader, High Performer, Momentum Leader)
- Book: "Predicting Personality" by CEO Drew D'Agostino
- ROI calculator for teams
- Enterprise trust signals (Adobe, Verizon, Amazon logos)
- Communication coaching ("How to communicate with a D-type", etc.)

### Weaknesses

- **Free tier is very thin.** Just basic DISC type and a few trait descriptions. No depth without paying.
- **DISC-only in free tier.** Enneagram, 16P, and Big Five tests exist but are secondary. The product is built around DISC.
- **Personality predictions feel invasive.** The "predict anyone's personality" feature raises privacy concerns. Some users find it stalker-ish.
- **B2B positioning excludes casual users.** The site feels corporate. "For Sales", "For Hiring" — not speaking to individuals exploring their personality.
- **Opaque team pricing.** No public pricing for business plans. Must request demo.
- **Limited free content.** Blog is sparse compared to Truity. Educational content about DISC is good but narrow.
- **Predictions require LinkedIn data.** Limits the social sharing / viral loop potential.

### SEO Position

- Strong for "DISC test", "DISC personality test", "DISC assessment", "best personality test for work"
- Ranks for workplace/communication queries: "how to communicate with [type]", "DISC types explained"
- Owns "personality test for sales", "personality test for hiring" commercial intent keywords
- Moderate blog content covering DISC, Enneagram, 16P, Big Five educational topics
- G2 profile provides review-driven SEO for "Crystal knows" branded queries

### Opportunity for 1Test

- **Individual user focus.** Crystal is B2B-first. 1Test can own the individual user who wants to understand themselves, not just sell better.
- **Multi-framework vs DISC-only.** Crystal's core is DISC. 1Test offers 4 frameworks. An Enneagram seeker won't find what they need on Crystal.
- **Transparent pricing.** Crystal hides team pricing. 1Test can publish clear pricing from day one.
- **Ethical positioning on predictions.** 1Test should never predict someone's personality without their consent. This is a trust differentiator. Crystal's "predict anyone" feature could become a liability.
- **Target "free DISC test" + "free Enneagram test" + "free Strengths test".** Crystal ranks for DISC keywords but not the others. 1Test can capture multi-framework search traffic.
