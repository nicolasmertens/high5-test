# 1Test.me — Strategy Document, April 17 2026

## The Business

1Test.me is a personality intelligence platform. One free 120-question test derives four frameworks (Strengths, 16 Personalities, DISC, Enneagram). Live at https://1test.me, React/Vite/TypeScript on Vercel. Repo: github.com/nicolasmertens/1test.

## The Insight

The test is a protocol, not a product. Like Stripe is a payment protocol — you don't change the checkout, you change what's around it. The 120 questions never change. What changes is the post-results experience, which branches based on who sent the person there and what stage of life they're in.

## Revenue Model

### Consumer (1test.me direct)
- Free: Top 5 strengths + teaser of other frameworks
- $9-15: Full profile (all 20 strengths + detailed MBTI/DISC/Enneagram + PDF export)
- $19: AI-generated personalized playbook (~$0.02/gen cost)
- $29/mo: Team features (invite colleagues, relationship reports, team dashboard)
- Affiliate: Book/course recommendations based on profile type
- No ads during the test

### B2B Recruiter Channel
- $400/mo per seat (or per-assessment pricing TBD)
- Branded link or embed — candidate takes standard test
- Candidate sees clean summary only, no career advice or competitor links until recruiter approves
- Recruiter dashboard: candidate comparison, team fit analysis, interview question suggestions
- Design partner: Nick's recruiter buddy — build for him first

### Future B2B Channels
- Universities: sold to career services, student-focused "what's next?" framing
- Executive coaches/consultants: white-labeled with their branding, per-seat licensing
- Corporate HR: team dynamics, hiring pipelines, internal mobility

## Architecture Principle: Context Branching

Every test session has a context: who sent this person here? The test is identical. The post-results experience branches:

- **Consumer**: full results, books, communities, career advice, soft upsells, viral sharing
- **Recruiter**: gated results, recruiter gets dashboard, no career advice until approved
- **University**: entry-level career paths, study group matching, alumni links
- **Coach**: co-branded, full profile for session use

This means the frontend must be a component/block system, not hardcoded pages. Results page assembles from blocks based on context. New audience = new block config, not new code.

## Audience Segments (same test, different "what's next?")

The test already reveals personality. Add 2-3 intake questions (age range, career stage, reason for taking) and the post-results page personalizes:

- **University students (18-24)**: "What's next?" = survival and status. Job search tactics, interview prep, "your strengths in a cover letter" template, entry-level career paths.
- **Early career (25-32)**: Direction and growth. Industry fit, skill gap analysis, networking strategy based on personality type.
- **Plateaued professionals (30-45)**: Management track vs. specialist track. Leadership gap analysis, soft skill development, executive coaching recommendations.
- **Midlife (40s)**: Authenticity. "Who am I really?" Identity beyond job title. Career pivot analysis, values alignment.
- **55+**: Legacy. Mentorship opportunities, portfolio career suggestions, what to pass on.
- **Solopreneurs/founders**: Operational blind spots. "Your first hire should be..." analysis, complementary co-founder profile, integrator vs. visionary framing.

## The Post-Results Page ("What's Next?")

This is where the business lives. After seeing top 5 strengths, the user sees blocks in this order:

1. Unlock full profile — all 20 strengths + 4 frameworks ($9, primary upsell)
2. Books you'll love — 3-5 affiliate picks based on type combo (free, earns affiliate rev)
3. Career paths that fit — personalized by audience segment (free teaser, deep analysis paid)
4. Communities for you — Reddit, Facebook, Discord links curated by type (free, builds goodwill)
5. Compare with a friend — share link, see compatibility when they take it (free, viral loop)
6. Match with LinkedIn — connect profile, career alignment analysis (paid, high perceived value)
7. Download your results — branded PDF (paid tier)
8. AI playbook — personalized action plan generated by Claude ($19, high margin)

Half of this is free. The free stuff drives sharing and return visits. The paid stuff sits naturally beside it.

## Viral Loop

Take test → see results → share card on social → invite 3 colleagues free → relationship report requires both profiles → team dashboard at 5+ people. Shareable result cards (PNG/OG image) are critical infrastructure.

## Multi-Language

Not a feature — a growth multiplier. German, French, Spanish, Portuguese first. Each language gets its own SEO landing pages ("/de/disc-test", "/fr/test-enneagramme"). The personality test keyword space is high-volume and beatable in non-English markets.

## Operating Principles

- Ship outward, not inward. Agent time should produce artifacts users or customers see, not internal coordination documents.
- The recruiter is the first customer. Build for him before optimizing the consumer funnel.
- Free value earns trust. Not everything is a paywall. Books are affiliate. Community links are free. Career advice is freemium. Generosity at scale is the moat.
- Multi-language is a multiplier, not a nice-to-have. Every feature built should be i18n-ready from day one.
- Context branching is architecture. Build the block system early so every new audience is configuration, not code.
